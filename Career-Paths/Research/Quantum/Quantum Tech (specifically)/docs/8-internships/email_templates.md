# Email Templates for Research and Internship Outreach

Cold-emailing professors and researchers is a standard and effective way to secure research internships in Quantum Technology. Well-written emails demonstrate clarity, seriousness, and respect for the recipient’s time.

This section provides **practical templates** you can adapt.

---

## General guidelines before emailing

Before sending any email:
- Read the professor’s recent papers
- Understand the lab’s research focus
- Identify how your interests align
- Keep the email concise and specific

Never send generic mass emails.

---

## Subject line examples

Use clear and relevant subject lines:
- Prospective Undergraduate Research Intern (Quantum Computing)
- Inquiry Regarding Summer Research Opportunity in Quantum Optics
- Undergraduate Student Interested in Quantum Algorithms Research

Avoid vague subjects.

---

## Template 1: General research internship inquiry
Subject: Prospective Undergraduate Research Intern – Quantum Computing

Dear Professor [Last Name],

I am an undergraduate student in Engineering Physics at [Your Institution], currently in my [year] year. I have a strong interest in quantum computing, particularly in [specific area], and I have been studying your group’s work on [specific paper/topic].

I have experience with [relevant skills, tools, or projects], and I am eager to gain research exposure under your guidance. I would be grateful for the opportunity to contribute to your group as a research intern during [time period].

I have attached my CV and included links to relevant projects below for your reference.

Thank you for your time and consideration.

Sincerely,  
[Your Name]  
[Institution]  
[Your Name]


## Template 2: Follow-up email (after no response)
Subject: Follow-up: Undergraduate Research Inquiry

Dear Professor (Last Name),

I hope you are doing well. I am writing to follow up on my previous email regarding the possibility of a research internship in your group.

I remain very interested in your work on [specific topic], and I would be grateful for the opportunity to discuss potential ways I could contribute.

Thank you again for your time.

Best regards,  
[Your Name]  
Wait at least 10–14 days before following up.

---

## Template 3: Email after reading a paper
Subject: Question Regarding Your Work on [Paper Title]

Dear Professor [Last Name],

I am an undergraduate student in Engineering Physics at [Your Institution]. I recently read your paper titled “[Paper Title]” and found your approach to [specific aspect] particularly interesting.

I am currently working on [brief description of your work], and your paper has been very helpful in shaping my understanding. I was wondering if you might have any advice on [specific question] or whether there might be opportunities to engage with your group.

Thank you for your time.

Sincerely,  
[Your Name]

This approach often receives higher response rates.

---

## What to attach or link

Always include:
- CV (PDF)
- GitHub profile
- Relevant project repositories
- Blog posts or reports (optional)

Do not attach large files.

---

## Common mistakes to avoid

- Writing long emails
- Using informal language
- Copy-pasting without customization
- Asking vague questions
- Attaching transcripts unnecessarily

Professionalism matters.

---

## If you receive a rejection or no reply

- Do not take it personally
- Thank them politely if they reply
- Continue applying elsewhere
- Improve your profile and try again

Persistence is part of research culture.

---

## Final note

A thoughtful email reflects your seriousness and preparation.

Good outreach combined with a strong profile can open doors to excellent research opportunities in Quantum Technology.
