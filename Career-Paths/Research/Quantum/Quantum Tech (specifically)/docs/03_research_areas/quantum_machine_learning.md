# Quantum Machine Learning (QML)

Quantum Machine Learning explores how quantum computing can be combined with machine learning to improve learning algorithms, optimization, or data processing.

This field sits at the intersection of **quantum computing, classical machine learning, and optimization**, and is one of the most application-oriented quantum research areas.

---

## What is Quantum Machine Learning?

Quantum Machine Learning investigates:
- How quantum computers can accelerate or enhance ML tasks
- How classical ML can help design and control quantum systems
- Hybrid quantumâ€“classical learning models

Most current QML approaches are hybrid, using classical computers alongside small quantum circuits.

---

## Key approaches in QML

Common directions include:
- Variational quantum circuits for classification and regression
- Quantum kernels and feature maps
- Quantum-enhanced optimization
- Quantum-inspired classical algorithms
- Using ML for noise mitigation and hardware control

True quantum advantage in ML remains an open research question.

---

## Why QML is attractive to students

- Lower entry barrier compared to pure theory
- Strong overlap with classical ML skills
- Direct relevance to industry applications
- Easier transition to non-quantum ML roles if needed

This makes QML a popular entry point for students.

---

## Skills required

### Core prerequisites
- Linear algebra
- Probability and statistics
- Basic quantum mechanics
- Optimization theory

### Machine learning skills
- Supervised and unsupervised learning
- Neural networks
- Gradient-based optimization
- Model evaluation

### Tools
- Python
- Qiskit Machine Learning
- PennyLane
- TensorFlow / PyTorch
- NumPy and SciPy

---

## Suitable student projects

- Implement variational classifiers
- Compare quantum vs classical kernels
- Study noise effects on QML models
- Apply QML to small datasets
- Use ML for quantum error mitigation

Projects should focus on **critical evaluation**, not just performance claims.

---

## Research and industry relevance

QML research is active in:
- Industry research labs
- Quantum startups
- Academic ML + quantum groups

However, most roles still require strong **classical ML expertise**.

---

## Career paths

Students specializing in QML may pursue:
- Quantum applications engineer roles
- Research roles in quantum software
- PhD programs in QML or applied quantum computing
- Hybrid ML + quantum industry roles

QML also provides a strong fallback into classical ML careers.

---

## Who should choose this area?

This area is suitable if you:
- Enjoy coding and experimentation
- Have interest in machine learning
- Prefer applied research
- Want flexibility between quantum and classical careers

QML rewards practical skills and careful evaluation over hype.
