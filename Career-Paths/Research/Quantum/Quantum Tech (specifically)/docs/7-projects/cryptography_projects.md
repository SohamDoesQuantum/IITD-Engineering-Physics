# Quantum Cryptography Projects

Quantum cryptography projects focus on secure communication using quantum mechanical principles. These projects are among the most mature and application-ready areas of Quantum Technology, making them highly relevant for industry and government roles.

This section outlines **how students can build strong, realistic quantum cryptography projects**.

---

## Why quantum cryptography projects matter

Quantum cryptography projects:
- Have clear real-world relevance
- Are conceptually grounded in physics
- Can be implemented via simulation
- Are valued in security, defence, and communication roles

They bridge theory, software, and applied systems.

---

## Core areas in quantum cryptography

Key subdomains include:
- Quantum Key Distribution (QKD)
- Quantum-safe communication protocols
- Eavesdropping and attack models
- Noise and loss analysis
- Device-independent cryptography (advanced)

QKD is the most accessible starting point.

---

## Beginner-level projects

Suitable for early exposure:
- Simulate BB84 protocol
- Implement E91 protocol
- Study error rates and key generation
- Analyze eavesdropping detection
- Compare ideal vs noisy channels

Focus on protocol logic and security reasoning.

---

## Intermediate-level projects

Require deeper understanding:
- Noise and loss modelling in QKD
- Finite-key analysis
- Attack simulations
- Protocol efficiency comparison
- Classical post-processing steps

These projects show analytical depth.

---

## Advanced-level projects

For late-stage undergraduates:
- Device-independent QKD simulations
- Quantum network security models
- Integration with classical cryptography
- Performance benchmarking under realistic assumptions

Advanced projects should emphasize assumptions and limitations.

---

## Skills required

- Quantum mechanics (measurement and states)
- Probability and statistics
- Python programming
- Basic cryptographic concepts
- Linear algebra

Optics knowledge is useful but not mandatory for simulations.

---

## Tools commonly used

- Python
- Qiskit
- QuTiP
- NumPy and SciPy
- Custom simulators

Choose tools that support clarity and reproducibility.

---

## Documentation standards

Each cryptography project should include:
- Clear protocol description
- Threat model
- Assumptions and limitations
- Results and interpretation
- Security analysis

Security claims must be precise.

---

## Career relevance

Quantum cryptography projects are relevant for:
- Secure communication companies
- Defence and national labs
- Quantum networking roles
- Cybersecurity-focused quantum startups

They offer strong application alignment.

---

## Common mistakes to avoid

- Overstating security claims
- Ignoring realistic noise and loss
- Copying protocol descriptions without analysis
- Confusing classical and quantum security assumptions

Precision matters in cryptography.

---

## Final note

Quantum cryptography rewards careful reasoning and honesty.

Well-executed projects in this area signal maturity, rigor, and strong application awareness in Quantum Technology.
